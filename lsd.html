~ Psychedelic ~

Other Names: LSD, Acid, Blotters

Background: Created by Albert Hofmann in 1938, popularized as a recreational drug in the 1960s counterculture.

Effects & Risks

  • Euphoria, music enhancement
  • Anxiety (especially at higher doses)
  • Visual hallucinations, pupil dilation
  • Insomnia, nausea
  • Long Term: Cognitive and mental health issues (rare)

Fatal Overdose Risk: Extremely low

Duration

  • Duration: 8 – 12 hours
  • Onset: 30 – 90 minutes
  • Peak: 2 – 5 hours
  • Plateau: 5 – 8 hours
  • Comedown: 8 – 12 hours

Doses

  • Threshold: 40 – 75µg
  • Standard: 75 – 150µg
  • High: 200 – 400µg
  • Heroic: 400µg+

*Doses depend on tolerance*

Other Information

LSD is a potent psychedelic known for its stimulating effects and vivid hallucinations. It gained cultural attention in the 1960s, often tied to music and visual art scenes.

Typically consumed on blotter paper (~¼" squares), though liquid and gelatin forms exist. Higher doses can be overwhelming, leading to confusion or ego loss.

WARNING: LSD is rarely adulterated, but some dangerous compounds like 25I-NBOMe have been misrepresented as LSD. If it has a bitter or metallic taste, do not take it.

Substance Mixing

Dangerous / Avoid

  • Antidepressants: Can cause serotonin syndrome or dull LSD's effects.
    Examples: Fluoxetine, Sertraline, Venlafaxine
  • Other stimulants: Increases heart risk and mental strain.
    Examples: Cocaine, Amphetamines (Adderall, Meth), high-dose Caffeine

Safe / Caution

  • Cannabis: Often intensifies visuals or helps calm anxiety.
    Example: Weed
  • Other psychedelics: Intensify mental effects. Best for experienced users.
    Examples: Psilocybin, 2C-B, Peyote
  • Empathogens: May synergize well, but increase serotonin load.
    Examples: MDMA, MDA, 6-APB
  • Dissociatives: Can be disorienting at high doses.
    Examples: DXM, PCP, Ketamine
