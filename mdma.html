    • MDMA

    [Ecstasy, Molly]

    ~Empathogen~/pre>


    Background:
    

    Created in 1912, popular as a street drug by the 1980s.
    


    Effects & Risks:
    

      
  
    • Intense euphoria, stimulation, increased sociability
      • 
  
    • Insomnia, nausea, jaw clenching, pupil dilation
      • 
  
    • Serotonin syndrome
      • 
  
    • Long Term: Cognitive and mental health issues, serotonin depletion, heart issues, has shown potential for PTSD treatment though.
      • 
  
    • Fatal Overdose Risk: Low
      • 

    


    Duration:
    

    
  Onset: 30–60 minutes
    
  Peak: 1.5–3 hours
    
  Comedown: 3–6 hours
    
  Total Duration: 3–6 hours

    


    Doses:
    

      
  
    • Threshold: 30–50mg
      • 
  
    • Standard: 75–125mg
      • 
  
    • High: 125–160mg
      • 
  
    • Redosing: 40–60mg (only redose after 1.5–2 hours)
      • 

    

    *Note: Redosing increases neurotoxicity, decreases euphoria*
    
*Dosing depends on body weight and tolerance*
    


    Other Information:
    

    
  MDMA is a powerful stimulant that usually makes people want to socialize, making it a really popular choice as a party drug. MDMA comes in different forms but the most common are pills and powder — pills are orally ingested, and powder is insufflated.

    

    
  While rolling, you can feel hot or dehydrated, so make sure to drink water — but not too much. Comedowns are difficult and usually involve a hard depressive crash. Due to the large amounts of serotonin MDMA releases, it's recommended to only take Molly every 4–6+ weeks to maintain healthy serotonin levels.

    

    **MDMA IS FREQUENTLY UNPURE AND USUALLY CONTAINS OTHER SUBSTANCES LIKE OPIOIDS OR METHAMPHETAMINE**
    


    Substance Mixing:
    


    Dangerous / Avoid
    

      
  
    • Most antidepressants (e.g., Fluoxetine, Sertraline, Venlafaxine) can block MDMA or cause serotonin syndrome (can be fatal)
      • 
  
    • Most other stimulants (e.g., Cocaine, Adderall, Meth, high caffeine) increase heart stress and risk of fatality
      • 
  
    • Other empathogens (e.g., MDA, 6-APB, MBDB, Ethylone/Methylone) increase neurotoxicity and serotonin syndrome risk
      • 

    


    Safe / Caution
    

      
  
    • Cannabis: Generally safe but may increase paranoia or intensity
      • 
  
    • Psychedelics: Safe to mix, but intensity/paranoia may occur (use caution and experience)
      • 
  
    • Benzodiazepines: Safe for comedown or anxiety, but not for enhancing a roll
      •
